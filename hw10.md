###Exercise 17C-25
Find a polynomial f (x) of degree ≤ 2 so that f (−1) = 1, f (0) = 0, f (1) = 2.

The auxiliary polynomial is
$$m(x)=(x+1)(x-1)x = x^3 - x$$
$$m'(x)=3x^2 - 1$$
Note, $m'(-1)=2$, $m'(0)=-1$, $m'(1)=2$.  
So,
$$e_1(x)={1\over 2}(x-1)x$$
$$e_2(x)=-(x-1)(x+1)$$
$$e_3(x)={1\over 2}(x+1)x$$

So finally,
$$f(x)=e_1(x)+2e_3(x)$$
$$f(x)={1\over 2}(x-1)x + (x+1)x$$
